# Limitations

The scenes are synthetic and small relative to real manipulation, even with the added stress, dense-object, extreme 10/12-object, benchmark-style synthetic task-suite, deployment-gate policy simulation, learned domain-shift, learned selection transfer, learned repair-policy transfer, domain-randomized, counterfactual target-swap, target-identity sweep, pilot-calibration, pilot-budget, leave-one-failure-out calibration, noisy-probe reliability, and probe-cost sensitivity panels. The learned model is a CPU NumPy semi-learned artifact, not a modern large-scale object-centric model. The observable-only, pilot-calibrated, noisy-probe, probe-cost, learned repair-policy, and deployment-gate policy panels reduce direct dependence on hidden-property truth alignment and free-probe assumptions, but all diagnostic probes and slot signals are still generated by the toy environment. The target-swap, target-sweep, learned-selection transfer, learned repair-policy transfer, deployment-policy, and synthetic task-suite panels catch fixed-target, disconnected-learned-artifact, untested-learned-repair, untested-gate, and isolated-stress assumptions but remain controlled synthetic tests. The toy model-family proxy panel is diagnostic only and does not replace independent graph-physics, latent dynamics, or diffusion world-model benchmarks. There is no real-robot evidence and no broad benchmark suite.

The paper-readiness target is therefore controlled synthetic v1. Broader claims require external environments, stronger learned models, and independent benchmarks.
