# Limitations

The scenes are synthetic and small relative to real manipulation, even with the added stress, dense-object, extreme 10/12-object, learned domain-shift, domain-randomized, counterfactual target-swap, pilot-calibration, leave-one-failure-out calibration, and noisy-probe reliability panels. The learned model is a CPU NumPy semi-learned artifact, not a modern large-scale object-centric model. The observable-only, pilot-calibrated, and noisy-probe repair panels reduce direct dependence on hidden-property truth alignment, but all diagnostic probes and slot signals are still generated by the toy environment. The target-swap panel catches fixed-target assumptions but remains a controlled synthetic retargeting test. The toy model-family proxy panel is diagnostic only and does not replace independent graph-physics, latent dynamics, or diffusion world-model benchmarks. There is no real-robot evidence and no broad benchmark suite.

The paper-readiness target is therefore controlled synthetic v1. Broader claims require external environments, stronger learned models, and independent benchmarks.
