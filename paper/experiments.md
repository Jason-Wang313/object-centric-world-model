# Experiments

Experiment A studies controlled object-binding failures across good, swap, merge/split, occlusion, hidden-property, and raw combined scenarios.

Experiment B trains and evaluates the CPU NumPy semi-learned object-centric model on generated trajectories.

Experiment C stresses occlusion and identity persistence with crossing target/distractor trajectories.

Experiment D tests hidden-property probing through diagnostic push, tap, lift, and observe actions.

Experiment E evaluates the combined repair stack against raw, random, and oracle selectors.

Experiment F runs a high-N stress panel for raw, occlusion, hidden-property, swap, and merge/split scenarios.

Experiment G reports learned-model learning curves for hidden-property accuracy, identity-alignment accuracy, and transition MSE.

Experiment H reports repair ablations, comparing raw, the best single repair, the combined repair, and oracle.

Experiment I reports seed-block robustness for the raw selected-tail failure and repair gain.

Experiment J reports raw-score calibration and score-noise sensitivity for raw and combined repair selectors.

Experiment K reports a good-scene negative control and learned feature ablations.

Experiment L reports dense-object OOD stress on 6- and 8-object synthetic scenes.

The required figures and upgraded evidence figures are generated by `experiments/run_experiments.py`.
