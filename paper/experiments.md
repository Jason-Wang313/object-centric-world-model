# Experiments

Experiment A studies controlled object-binding failures across good, swap, merge/split, occlusion, hidden-property, and raw combined scenarios.

Experiment B trains and evaluates the CPU NumPy semi-learned object-centric model on generated trajectories.

Experiment C stresses occlusion and identity persistence with crossing target/distractor trajectories.

Experiment D tests hidden-property probing through diagnostic push, tap, lift, and observe actions.

Experiment E evaluates the combined repair stack against raw, random, and oracle selectors.

Experiment F runs a high-N stress panel for raw, occlusion, hidden-property, swap, and merge/split scenarios.

Experiment G reports learned-model learning curves for hidden-property accuracy, identity-alignment accuracy, and transition MSE.

Experiment G2 reports learned-model held-out domain-shift checks on dense, occluded, crossing, and mixed object-count synthetic variants.

Experiment H reports repair ablations, comparing raw, the best single repair, the combined repair, and oracle.

Experiment I reports seed-block robustness for the raw selected-tail failure and repair gain.

Experiment J reports raw-score calibration and score-noise sensitivity for raw and combined repair selectors.

Experiment K reports a good-scene negative control and learned feature ablations.

Experiment L reports dense-object OOD stress on 6- and 8-object synthetic scenes.

Experiment M reports controlled toy model-family proxy diagnostics for latent-global, relational-slot, and diffusion-score selectors.

Experiment N reports nonparametric bootstrap confidence checks for the main controlled failure, repair, OOD, and toy-proxy effects.

Experiment O reports an observable-only repair stress test that avoids direct hidden-property truth alignment in the selector score.

Experiment P reports held-out domain-randomized synthetic stress with varied object count, occlusion, hidden-property, and crossing flags.

Experiment Q reports counterfactual target-swap stress where the true target is retargeted from object 0 to object 1, exercising target identity rather than assuming a fixed target ID.

Experiment R reports pilot-label calibration: a small ridge selector is trained on labeled candidate futures from separate seeds and evaluated on held-out raw, randomized-domain, and target-swap scenes.

Experiment S reports leave-one-failure-out pilot calibration: each raw, occlusion, hidden-property, swap, or merge/split failure family is excluded from pilot-calibrator training and evaluated as the held-out test family.

Experiment T reports noisy-probe reliability stress: the diagnostic hidden-property observation is corrupted across a reliability sweep, and the noisy-probe repair score is evaluated without direct hidden-property truth alignment.

Experiment U reports extreme object-count stress on 10- and 12-object synthetic scenes, with a separate good control and corrupted occlusion/hidden-property variants.

Experiment V reports probe-cost sensitivity: diagnostic-action costs are subtracted before selected real utility is reported, testing whether probe-based repair remains useful when probes are not free.

Experiment W reports pilot-label budget sensitivity: the pilot utility calibrator is trained on deterministic labeled-candidate subsets and evaluated on the same held-out raw, randomized-domain, and target-swap families.

The required figures and upgraded evidence figures are generated by `experiments/run_experiments.py`.
