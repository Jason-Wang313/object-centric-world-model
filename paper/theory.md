# Theory

The theory section formalizes score-tail selection over a finite scored object-candidate population. Score tie groups are sorted from high to low. The probability of selecting a tie group is the probability that all N samples come from that group or lower, minus the probability that all N samples come from lower groups only.

This yields an exact expected selected utility. The same expression handles real-valued utility and binary success. Object-centric structure enters through the candidate definitions: score is an object-model score, while utility is measured on true target identity and physical success.
