# Differentiation From Best-of-N WAM

This repository intentionally keeps the finite Best-of-N law as an abstract selector theorem while changing the scientific object.

What is shared:

- A finite best-of-n law for selected utility under score-based selection.
- Tie-aware handling of equal scores.
- Monte Carlo validation of the finite law.
- Conservative claim auditing and command ergonomics.

What is different:

- The candidates are object-centric futures with slots, identities, hidden properties, and trajectories.
- The failures are target/distractor swaps, occlusion persistence errors, hidden-property mistakes, and merge/split artifacts.
- Repairs are object-specific: temporal identity consistency, property posterior updates, targeted probes, and combined object repair.
- The experiments are synthetic object manipulation scenes, not WAM environments or WAM failure narratives.

No WAM result is claimed here. The finite law pattern is the reusable mathematical shell; the research question is object binding under Best-of-N world-model inference.
